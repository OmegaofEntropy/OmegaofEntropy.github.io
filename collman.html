Collections Management Support

This is where I offer support for collections stewardship through information and the production of supplies such as labels and specimen placeholders. I find that the proper care of collections supports museum outreach efforts by providing a larger selection of useful and informational specimens to be used in activities, exhibits, workshops, kits, and general teaching. It also promotes research, which in turn can support outreach and educational efforts.

Supplies provided here are free for download, use, and distribution as long as the following conditions are met:

  • The author's name and all information remains visible on the work.
  • When applicable, the name(s) and/or information of the organization for which the material was created remains visible on the work.

Collections Management Database Reviews

 A wide array of databases are used by different institutions. If you are considering switching databases for your collection or starting a database from scratch, this page will give you an overview of the most important qualities of several databases commonly used in collections management.

Object Removal Cards

 When a specimen is removed from storage or from an exhibit for use or loan, the safest thing to do in order to preserve its informational integrity is to put an informational card in its place during its absence. Download some for your institution here.

Specimen Labels

 Here I provide various labels to be used with natural history collections. Some labels point out radioactivity, others draw attention to the presence of arsenic in the specimen.
